Meet Our Lab Members and Students



Ms. Alberta Serwah Anning (Left) (PhD Candidate, Parasitology)

Co-Investigator

Ms. Anning has worked extensively on Leishmania parasites and has co-authored several papers in reputable journals. She manages The Ghartey-Kwansah Lab and The Department of Biomedical Sciences Lab.

Mr. Emmanuel Adu Sarpong, MPhil student (Pathogen and Vector Biology)

Mr. Emmanuel holds a Bachelor's in Biomedical Science from the University of Cape Coast. Currently, he is a graduate student and research assistant in the GGK Lab with interests in bioinformatics, pathogen genomics, molecular biology, and infectious diseases.

Mr. Pius Kwesi Sam (BSc. Biomedical Sciences, 2022)

Pius Kwasi Sam is a research assistant in the GGK lab with interests in molecular and cellular biology, bioinformatics, and computational drug discovery. His research interests specifically lies within understanding disease mechanisms and therapeutic inhibitory studies. He aims to uncover the molecular connections between neglected tropical diseases (NTDs) and non-communicable diseases such as cancer and neuropathologies, contributing to drug discovery and therapeutic development.
